Core Frameworks

Not Theory. Operating Systems.

Every framework is built from real weight rooms — tested, refined, and ready to deploy.

Framework 01

Exercise Selection Funnel

Most coaches pick exercises based on what looks good or what they've always done. The Exercise Selection Funnel changes that. Every exercise decision runs through the same filter: primary joint, movement pattern, intent, best exercise for that intent, volume, and load. No guessing. No randomness. Just a repeatable system that builds consistent programming every single time.

Framework 02

FYOP — Full-Year Optimization Plan

Most programs react to the schedule. FYOP builds ahead of it. The Full-Year Optimization Plan maps every month of the year to a specific training phase before the year starts — so your athletes are always in the right phase at the right time, no matter what the sport calendar throws at you. Stop winging the year. Start owning it.

Framework 03

Core 4 Movement Prep

Long warm-ups kill momentum and waste time. Core 4 cuts it down to what actually matters — four loadable movements that hit every major joint system: hips, spine, and shoulders. These aren't just feel-good stretches. They're progressive, trackable, and designed to improve over time. The best prep system is one athletes actually do — and this one works.

Framework 04

Athlete Buy-In Rate (ABIR)

Buy-in isn't random. It's the result of specific inputs: how good the session experience is, what results athletes are chasing, how you coach them, and how much friction exists in the environment. ABIR puts a formula to it — P × R^I − DP — so you can stop guessing why athletes are checked out and start fixing the actual problem. Measure it. Manage it. Change it.

Framework 05

L3 Method

Every athlete needs three things: sport skill, physical development, and cardiovascular capacity. The L3 Method organizes training into three layers — Skill, Physical, and Cardiovascular — and tells you exactly how to sequence and prioritize them based on where the athlete is in their year. No more random blocks. No more missed qualities. One framework, full-year clarity.

Framework 06

The Coach's Role

Most new coaches walk into the weight room thinking they're the main character. They're not. The strength coach's job is to support the sport — to develop what the sport demands and stay out of the way of everything else. Understanding your role doesn't shrink your impact. It focuses it. And focused coaches build the programs that athletes, sport coaches, and schools actually trust.
